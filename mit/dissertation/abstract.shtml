Urban planning is inherently a multi-disciplinary pursuit. There is no one person or agency that can do it well. In order to steward our natural resources, economic future, and quality of life we draw upon the expertise of many fields. This has its advantages, but it makes the synthesis of analyses difficult, and leaves little ability for decision-makers to try out different scenarios or change the underlying assumptions to account for new information.

This research presents a framework for a new paradigm of modeling and information infrastructure based on new and emerging Internet technologies. The system achieves four goals:

  • Analytic models are made more visible and changeable.
  • Models from different groups can interact and be the basis of more holistic research.
  • System creation and maintenance are less costly than is now the norm.
  • Less well-funded and sophisticated organizations, such as small towns and community organizations, are able to participate in the system.

Particular attention is paid to how the quality and quantity of analysis can increase exponentially in this environment, and how the nature and use of analyses will evolve.  A prototype system is designed and built around the process of zoning and master planning that all municipalities go through on an ongoing basis.
