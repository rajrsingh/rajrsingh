Distributed and Collaborative Planning Analytics:
Land use planning in a networked society

 

Monday, February 23
4:30pm
CRN Classroom (map)

This research presents a framework for shared analysis based on a critical look at the information flows between government agencies, consultants, and stakeholders. The strategy achieves four goals:

  • The nature of analytic models is made more visible and therefore brought into the public discourse.
  • The infrastructure proposed lowers transaction costs, making reflection, repetition, and refinement more feasible.
  • Models from different groups can interact and be the basis of more holistic research.
  • Barriers between presentation, analysis and record-keeping tools are removed.

Many emerging information technologies are discussed, including XML, XML Schema, Semantic Web, Web services and Flash.
